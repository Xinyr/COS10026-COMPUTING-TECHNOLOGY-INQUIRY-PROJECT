Tools

Tools used in herbarium

Secataurs

a pair of pruning clippers for use with one hand. They are designed to make clean cuts on stems, branches, and flowers, helping to promote healthy growth, minimal the damage to plants and maintain the shape of plants.

There are two main types:

  1. Bypass secateurs
  2. Anvil secateurs

Bypass secateurs, which have a scissor-like action and are great for live stems while Anvil secateurs, which have a straight blade that cuts against a flat surface, suitable for dead wood. The one we used here are bypass secataur.

Vasculum

Vasculum is a botanical collecting bag used by plant collectors and botanists to store and transport specimens. It is mostly made with lightweight yet durable material such as aluminium and tin. Vasculum could be important for herbarium as it protect the specimens from sun's UV rays, raindrops and hot winds during transportation, delaying the withering of the specimens.

Herbarium Press

A herbarium press is a tool that is used to press and dry plant specimens for preservation and scientific study. The press normally consist of two wooden board, with straps or screws to hold them together tightly.

Acid-free paper

Acid-free paper is paper that, if infused in water, yields a neutral or basic pH (7 or slightly greater). It is also lignin and sulfur-free. It is choosen over normal paper due to it's neutral characteristic that is better for the specimens long term preservation.

Adhesive

Adhesive such as glue is used to hold specimens in place. It also helps ensure that specimens remain intact over time without degrading or causing discoloration for long term preservation.

Definitions:

UV rays
-Ultraviolet rays are a type of electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths shorter than visible light but longer than X-rays.
Lignin
-Lignin is a biopolymer that binds cellulose and hemicellulose fibers and provides stiffness to plants.

Did you know?

The Biggest Herbarium Gallery

The biggest herbarium gallery is the Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew, located in London, UK. The Kew Herbarium houses over 7 million specimens, featuring a vast array of plant specimens all around the world. The gallery was founded in 1840, having a history of nearly 200 years and since have been the sacred ground for herbarium.
